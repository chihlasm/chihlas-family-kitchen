## Design Context

### Users
Immediate and extended family members, ages 15-63. Mixed tech comfort levels. Primary use is **phone in the kitchen while cooking** (~75%), with laptop/tablet for meal planning (~25%). Mobile-first design is critical — interactions must work with wet/floury hands, glanceable text, and forgiving tap targets.

### Brand Personality
**Modern, happy, exciting.** The interface should feel like walking into a warm, lively kitchen — not a sterile recipe database. Comfort and joy, not efficiency-first utility. This is a family heirloom in digital form, not a productivity tool.

Three emotional goals: **happy, warm, comfort.**

### Anti-References
- **allrecipes.com** — cluttered, ad-heavy, impersonal, generic content-farm energy
- **AI slop** — gradient text, glassmorphism, cyan-on-dark, identical card grids, side-stripe borders, purple-blue palettes, generic drop shadows on rounded rectangles

### Aesthetic Direction
**Evolving from the current warm/earthy foundation toward something more lively and modern** while keeping the warmth. Think: a beautifully designed independent cookbook from a beloved neighborhood restaurant — not a corporate recipe platform, not a minimalist tech app.

- **Theme:** Light — kitchens are bright, cooking happens in daylight and under warm overhead light
- **Palette direction:** Keep the warm terracotta DNA but push toward more vibrancy and joy. The current palette is pleasant but safe — it needs more personality and pop. Tint neutrals warm. Use color to create energy without chaos.
- **Typography direction:** Current Lora + DM Sans pairing is competent but generic. Explore typefaces with more character — something that feels handpicked, not default. The display font should have personality and warmth; the body font should be clean, highly readable on mobile, and feel friendly.

### Design Principles

1. **Kitchen-first** — Every design decision filters through "does this work on a phone propped up on a counter with flour on your hands?" Large tap targets, high contrast, glanceable hierarchy.

2. **Joyful, not cute** — Happy and warm without being childish or precious. Color and typography should create energy and delight, not whimsy. Think confident and inviting, not twee.

3. **Family character** — This isn't a public product. It should feel personal, like it belongs to *this* family. Details that feel chosen with care, not generated by algorithm.

4. **Evolved warmth** — The terracotta/cream foundation is the DNA. Evolution means more vibrancy, more contrast, more typographic personality — not a different identity.

5. **Anti-slop** — Every visual choice must pass the test: "Would someone assume AI made this?" If yes, redesign. Distinctive > safe. Intentional > decorative.
